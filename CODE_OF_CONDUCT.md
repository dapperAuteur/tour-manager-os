# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Tour Manager OS is built for musicians and their teams. We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone — contributors, users, and community members — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Standards

### Expected Behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Focus on what is best for the community and the product
- Show empathy toward other community members
- Credit others for their contributions

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sharing proprietary code, credentials, or user data outside authorized channels
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — including the codebase, issue tracker, pull requests, community forums, and any other communication channel associated with Tour Manager OS. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## For Platform Users

Users of the Tour Manager OS platform (musicians, crew, venue contacts, fans) are also expected to:

- Respect other community members in forums and discussions
- Not abuse the feedback system or support channels
- Not attempt to access data belonging to other organizations
- Report security vulnerabilities responsibly through proper channels
- Respect content ownership and intellectual property of artists

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project team at conduct@tourmanageros.com. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter of an incident.

### Enforcement Actions

1. **Warning** — A private written warning with clarity about the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.
2. **Temporary Suspension** — Temporary loss of access to project spaces and communication channels.
3. **Permanent Ban** — Permanent removal from all project spaces.
